## Guidelines

For starters, we have the general expectation that your code is tested
thoroughly before PRing. We will test ourselves, however it's important to
test your work before submitting it as a PR to us.

Make sure your description includes the following:
- Authors/co-authors
- What your PR actually changes and why
- [If adding a new feature] Why is this feature useful? Who benefits from it?
- [If fixing something] Describe what the issue is you are trying to fix
- [If fixing something] Describe why your PR fixes this

Make sure all patches are neat and tidy, no unnecessary diff is there, and make
sure to try and follow the code-style of the surrounding source. That way the
code we are patching/adding is a lot more clean rather than a random sharp
change in code style amongst the rest of the consistent code style.

## The Contributor Role & In-Software Recognition

We have added this section due to the increasing amount of people specifiaclly
DMing our team asking specifically for the role and what it takes to get said
role and then making the minimal contributions necessary to acquire it. Quite
literally people have made PRs fixing typos in the README file or a 1 char typo
in the source and then demanding the role.

We want people to contribute to Canvas for the purpose of actually wanting to be
a part of our community and help to grow and improve the software, not so you can
get bragging rights with your friends that you have a Discord role with an open
source project.

This role is given at the discretion of our team, and will not be given to
anyone requesting this role. If you feel that you deserve this role by fitting
the criteria listed below and want to ask *politely* for the role, then that's
fine. But contacting the team to just ask what the minimum contributions
required for a role is and the second it is merged ask for the role will not get
you the role.

To get the role, you generally need to have made **meaningful** contributions
to our repositories like Horizon, Canvas, our website, docs, etc. We are sligthly
more lenient with how much you need to contribute depending on how active you
are in our community too.

Along with that, those who have this role, as of 26.2, will get added to a
contributors list in the Canvas software itself, showing a nod/token of
appreciation to those who have helped Canvas meaningfully, since Canvas
wouldn't be here without those talented outside collaborators.

Overall, this is a **gift** from the Canvas team to you, not something you can
just demand and request.
